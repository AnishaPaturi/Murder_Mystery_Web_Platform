



# **THE SILENT TRUTH**

### Part 1 – The Beginning

---

## Chapter 1

### The Morning Everything Changed

Monday mornings were always the same.

You woke up at 6:30 a.m. in your tiny apartment above Mrs. Wilson's bakery. The smell of fresh bread filled your room before your alarm even rang.

You stretched, made yourself a cup of coffee, and looked outside.

The streets of Ashbrook were quiet.

People greeted each other by name.

Children walked to school laughing.

Old couples watered their gardens.

Nothing ever happened here.

That was why everyone froze when the first police siren echoed through town.

You looked out of your window.

Three police cars raced toward the large mansion at the end of Oak Street.

Edward Langston's house.

Edward was the richest man in town.

He owned construction companies, restaurants, and half the shopping center.

People respected him.

Some feared him.

But everyone knew him.

You quickly got dressed and headed outside.

Half the town was already walking toward the mansion.

Police officers stopped everyone behind yellow tape.

"What happened?" someone asked.

An officer sighed.

"Mr. Langston is dead."

The crowd went silent.

Someone whispered,

"Murder."

---

## Chapter 2

### You Meet the Victim

You never knew Edward personally.

You had only met him once.

Three nights ago.

You worked part-time at GreenMart.

Around 8:15 p.m., Edward had ordered groceries.

Since another delivery driver called in sick, your manager asked you to deliver them.

You didn't want to.

The mansion was nearly twenty minutes away.

But extra money was extra money.

Edward himself answered the door.

He looked tired.

His shirt sleeves were rolled up.

Papers covered the table behind him.

"Long day?" you asked politely.

He smiled.

"The longest."

You handed him the groceries.

As you walked back toward your bike, you noticed a leather wallet lying beside the front steps.

You picked it up.

"Sir!"

Edward turned around.

"My wallet."

He looked surprised.

"I didn't even realize I'd dropped it."

He opened it, checked inside, then smiled.

"Thank you."

"No problem."

He reached into the wallet.

You stepped back.

"I don't need a tip."

Edward laughed.

"Good."

Instead of money, he handed you a business card.

"If you ever need work, call me."

You looked at the card.

Edward Langston.

Chairman.

Langston Group.

You slipped it into your pocket.

That was the last time anyone outside his family officially saw him alive.

---

## Chapter 3

### The Police Visit

Around noon there was a knock on your apartment door.

Two officers stood outside.

"Can we ask you a few questions?"

Your stomach tightened.

"What about?"

"You delivered groceries to Mr. Langston Friday evening."

You nodded.

They asked simple questions.

What time did you arrive?

Did Edward seem worried?

Did you notice anyone else?

Did anything seem strange?

You answered honestly.

"No."

One officer wrote everything down.

The other thanked you.

"If you remember anything, call us."

They left.

You closed the door.

Something didn't feel right.

Edward looked tired that night.

Not frightened.

Not angry.

Just...

stressed.

Why?

---

## Chapter 4

### Curiosity

Three days later the newspapers were full of stories.

Businessman Murdered.

Police Have No Suspects.

Investigation Continues.

According to the newspaper:

Edward died between 9:00 and 10:00 p.m.

He was struck once with a heavy marble statue.

Nothing had been stolen.

The security cameras had stopped working that evening because of electrical repairs.

There was no sign of forced entry.

Police believed Edward knew his killer.

Everyone in town had an opinion.

"It was his business partner."

"It was his son."

"It had to be robbers."

"The wife did it."

Every person had a different answer.

None had proof.

You folded the newspaper.

For some reason...

You couldn't stop thinking about it.

---

## Chapter 5

### A Simple Notebook

You bought a small notebook for fifty rupees from the stationery shop.

On the first page you wrote:

**Edward Langston Murder**

Then underneath,

**Facts**

• Murder between 9 and 10 p.m.

• Heavy marble statue.

• Cameras not working.

• No robbery.

• Victim knew murderer.

That was all.

No secret evidence.

No police reports.

Just public information.

You laughed.

"What am I even doing?"

Still...

You kept writing.

---

## Chapter 6

### The Mansion

The police tape was still around the property.

An officer stopped you before you reached the gate.

"Visitors aren't allowed."

"I understand."

You looked around anyway.

Large gardens.

Tall trees.

Stone paths.

A garage.

Nothing unusual.

As you turned to leave, someone called your name.

"You delivered groceries."

You looked back.

A woman stood near the front door.

She looked exhausted.

"I'm Olivia."

Edward's wife.

"I'm sorry for your loss."

She nodded.

"I remember you."

"You returned Edward's wallet."

"I did."

"He mentioned you that evening."

You blinked.

"He did?"

"He said there are still honest people in this town."

She forced a small smile.

Then walked back inside.

That sentence stayed in your head the entire walk home.

---

## Chapter 7

### A Visit to the Café

You decided to visit the café opposite the mansion.

If anyone noticed something...

It would be people who sat near the windows every day.

The owner recognized you.

"Coffee?"

"Please."

After a few minutes you asked,

"Were you here Friday night?"

He nodded.

"Busy night."

"Did you notice anything unusual?"

He thought for a while.

"Actually..."

"There was one thing."

Your heart skipped.

"A black car stayed parked outside the mansion for nearly an hour."

"Whose car?"

"No idea."

"Did anyone get out?"

"I was serving customers."

"When I looked again..."

"The car was gone."

You wrote it down.

Black car.

Unknown owner.

Not much.

But better than nothing.

---

## Chapter 8

### The Family

You started learning about Edward's family.

Olivia.

His wife.

Married twenty-seven years.

Daniel.

His son.

Thirty-one.

Loved expensive cars.

Known for gambling.

Emma.

His daughter.

Worked as an architect.

Rarely attended family events.

Everyone had stories.

Daniel owed people money.

Emma argued with Edward often.

Olivia had complete access to Edward's money if he died.

Any one of them could have done it.

Or none of them.

You refused to guess.

Not yet.

---

## Chapter 9

### The Funeral

The funeral took place on Thursday.

Almost the whole town attended.

You stood quietly at the back.

People cried.

Others whispered.

Some simply watched.

One by one people hugged the family.

Then something caught your attention.

An elderly woman approached the coffin.

She placed a white rose beside it.

She smiled gently.

Not sadly.

Not happily.

Just...

peacefully.

Someone beside you whispered,

"That's Mrs. Evelyn Moore."

"The nicest woman in Ashbrook."

"She and Edward were neighbors for years."

You looked at her.

Gray hair.

Warm smile.

Simple clothes.

She looked like everyone's grandmother.

She stayed only a minute before quietly leaving.

You barely thought about her again.

---

## Chapter 10

### The Mechanic

The next afternoon you visited Mike's Auto Repair.

The café owner couldn't identify the black car.

Maybe a mechanic could.

Mike scratched his beard.

"Black sedan?"

"Lots of those."

"Did anyone bring one in recently?"

He nodded.

"Victor Hayes."

Edward's business partner.

"Anything wrong with it?"

"Dead battery."

"When?"

Mike checked an old notebook.

"Friday morning."

"So it worked Friday night?"

"Should have."

Interesting.

Victor now became the first person connected to your investigation.

Still...

It wasn't evidence.

Only coincidence.

---

## Chapter 11

### The First Lie

You decided to speak with Victor.

His office overlooked the river.

He welcomed you politely.

"What can I do for you?"

"I've been trying to understand Edward's murder."

Victor sighed.

"So has everyone else."

"Were you with Edward Friday evening?"

"No."

"When did you last see him?"

"Thursday."

His answer came quickly.

Too quickly.

You thanked him and left.

As you walked toward your bike, the receptionist hurried outside.

"Excuse me."

"You forgot this."

She handed you your notebook.

"Thank you."

Before returning inside she smiled.

"Oh, and Mr. Hayes was here with Mr. Langston Friday afternoon."

You froze.

Victor had just lied.

Why?

---

## Chapter 12

### A New Name

That evening you wrote in your notebook.

**Victor lied.**

Not proof.

Just a fact.

Then you drew a timeline.

Friday

8:15 p.m. — You deliver groceries.

9:00–10:00 p.m. — Estimated murder.

Unknown black car.

Victor lies.

Known killer.

None.

Unknown visitor.

Unknown motive.

You leaned back.

This was harder than you imagined.

Every answer created two more questions.

You closed your notebook.

Outside, rain began falling softly against your window.

You had no idea that before the week ended...

someone would try to stop you from asking questions.

# **THE SILENT TRUTH**

## Part 2 – Every Answer Creates More Questions

---

# Chapter 13

### The First Anonymous Letter

The rain had not stopped since Friday.

You sat at the small table in your apartment, staring at your notebook. Victor Hayes had lied about seeing Edward on Friday. It wasn't proof of murder, but it was the first thing that didn't fit.

Your phone rang.

It was Mrs. Wilson, your landlady.

"There's someone downstairs asking for you."

You walked down the narrow staircase.

A young woman stood in the hallway.

It was Emma Langston.

Edward's daughter.

"I hope I'm not disturbing you."

"You aren't."

She held out a small envelope.

"My father kept this in his study. The police returned some personal papers yesterday."

"And?"

"I found your name written on the back."

You frowned.

"My name?"

She nodded.

"He wrote, 'Ask the grocery delivery boy if anything seemed unusual.'"

You felt your stomach tighten.

Edward had remembered you.

Emma handed you the envelope.

"It was found on his desk."

She walked away before you could ask another question.

You carefully opened it.

Inside was a single sheet of paper.

There was no name.

No address.

Only one sentence.

**"If you tell anyone, twenty years of lies will finally end."**

Nothing else.

You read it three times.

Twenty years.

Whatever Edward was dealing with...

it had started long before you met him.

---

# Chapter 14

### The Hidden Study

The next morning you visited the mansion.

Emma was waiting near the entrance.

"The police finished searching yesterday," she said.

"You can look around if you want. Maybe you'll notice something."

You hesitated.

"I don't want to interfere."

"You won't."

The house felt strangely empty.

The silence was heavier than before.

Emma led you to Edward's study.

Books covered every wall.

Awards and framed certificates hung neatly above a fireplace.

Everything looked ordinary.

Too ordinary.

As you looked around, something caught your eye.

One bookshelf had scratches on the wooden floor beneath it.

It looked like it had been moved many times.

"Has this always been here?" you asked.

Emma looked confused.

"I think so."

You gently pushed against the shelf.

It moved.

Behind it was a narrow wooden door.

Emma gasped.

"I've never seen that before."

The room behind it was tiny.

No windows.

Just a desk.

An old chair.

And several cardboard boxes.

Inside the boxes were newspapers dating back nearly thirty years.

Every newspaper had one story carefully cut out.

The headline was always different.

But every article mentioned the same place.

**Ashbrook Museum.**

---

# Chapter 15

### The Museum Robbery

You spent the afternoon at the town library reading old newspapers.

Finally you found it.

Twenty-two years ago.

Ashbrook Museum had been robbed.

A priceless diamond necklace disappeared.

The thief was never caught.

Several employees were questioned.

One of them...

Edward Langston.

At the time, Edward had worked as a night security guard.

The article said police found no evidence against him.

The case slowly disappeared.

You copied every detail into your notebook.

Why would Edward keep newspaper articles about a robbery that happened twenty-two years ago?

Unless...

the robbery still mattered.

---

# Chapter 16

### Someone Is Watching

That evening you walked home through Maple Street.

The sidewalks were almost empty.

Streetlights reflected off the wet road.

Halfway home you heard footsteps behind you.

You turned.

Nobody.

You kept walking.

Again.

Footsteps.

Slow.

Steady.

You stopped.

The footsteps stopped too.

Your heart beat faster.

You turned quickly.

Still no one.

Just a man walking his dog.

A woman carrying groceries.

An empty bus stop.

You laughed at yourself.

Maybe you were imagining things.

Then you reached your apartment.

On your front door...

someone had written one word with black marker.

**STOP.**

---

# Chapter 17

### Mrs. Evelyn's Kindness

As you stared at the door, a soft voice came from behind.

"Oh dear."

You turned around.

Mrs. Evelyn Moore stood there carrying a basket of homemade cookies.

"The children in the neighborhood must be playing another silly prank."

She smiled kindly.

"I'll help you clean it."

Before you could answer, she took out a damp cloth.

Within seconds the word disappeared.

"There."

She smiled.

"Nothing to worry about."

You thanked her.

She wished you a good evening and slowly walked back toward her house.

You watched her leave.

Such a kind woman.

Always helping everyone.

You made a mental note to bake her something in return someday.

---

# Chapter 18

### Daniel Langston

You finally decided to meet Edward's son.

Daniel lived in a luxury apartment downtown.

He answered the door wearing sunglasses even though it was cloudy.

"What do you want?"

"I'm trying to understand what happened to your father."

"The police already asked everything."

"I'm not the police."

"I noticed."

He folded his arms.

"Were you at the mansion Friday night?"

"No."

"Where were you?"

"At a casino."

"Can anyone confirm that?"

He hesitated.

"Probably."

Probably.

Not exactly convincing.

Then something caught your attention.

His right hand had a deep cut across two fingers.

"What happened?"

Daniel quickly hid his hand.

"Kitchen accident."

You nodded.

He ended the conversation.

As you walked away you wrote one sentence.

**Daniel is hiding something.**

---

# Chapter 19

### The Gardener

The mansion's gardener, James, had worked there for almost fifteen years.

Unlike Daniel, he welcomed you warmly.

"You think someone in the family did it?" he asked.

"I don't know."

James looked toward the garden.

"Mr. Langston wasn't perfect."

"What do you mean?"

"He had secrets."

"What kind?"

James shook his head.

"I promised never to talk."

You waited.

After almost a minute he sighed.

"Every Friday night someone visited."

"The same person?"

"I never saw clearly."

"Why not?"

"They always came through the side gate."

"Were they a man or woman?"

"I honestly couldn't tell."

That was new.

Edward had regular secret meetings.

Every Friday.

---

# Chapter 20

### Another Letter

Back at your apartment you found another envelope pushed under your door.

No stamp.

No address.

Inside was another message.

**"Some doors should never be opened."**

Nothing else.

This time you didn't throw it away.

You compared the handwriting with the first letter.

It looked identical.

The same slanted letters.

The same dark blue ink.

Whoever wrote one...

had written both.

---

# Chapter 21

### A Mistake

You returned to the library.

You wanted to learn more about the museum robbery.

The librarian smiled as you approached.

"Researching history?"

"A little."

She helped you find old newspaper records.

"You've been coming here often," she said warmly.

"I guess I have."

"If you ever need help finding something, just ask."

You thanked her.

She spent almost twenty minutes helping you search.

Before leaving you smiled.

"You're very kind."

She laughed softly.

"I've always loved helping people."

You left the library feeling grateful.

You never noticed the newspaper she quietly slipped back onto the shelf.

One page was missing.

The page that mentioned the museum robbery.

---

# Chapter 22

### Victor's Secret

The next day you visited Victor Hayes again.

This time he looked tired.

"I know you lied."

He looked at you for a long moment.

Finally he sighed.

"Fine."

"I met Edward Friday afternoon."

"Why lie?"

"Because we argued."

"About what?"

Victor rubbed his forehead.

"Our company."

"He wanted to tell the board about financial problems."

"And you didn't?"

"No."

"Did you kill him?"

Victor looked genuinely shocked.

"No."

"Then why hide the argument?"

"Because everyone already thinks I wanted him dead."

That made sense.

It wasn't proof he was innocent.

But it explained the lie.

---

# Chapter 23

### The Photograph

That evening Emma called.

"I found something."

You rushed to the mansion.

She showed you an old family photograph.

Edward stood beside several people in front of Ashbrook Museum.

One face caught your attention.

A young woman smiling beside Edward.

The name written on the back was faded.

Only the first name remained.

**Evelyn.**

You frowned.

Could it be...

Mrs. Evelyn Moore?

Emma looked confused.

"You know her?"

"She's our neighbor."

Emma nodded.

"She visited Dad every Christmas."

You stared at the photograph.

A neighbor.

A friend.

Someone Edward had known for over twenty years.

Interesting.

But probably nothing.

---

# Chapter 24

### The Missing Notebook

You reached your apartment around 10 p.m.

The front door was unlocked.

You were certain you had locked it.

Slowly, you stepped inside.

Nothing looked disturbed.

Your laptop was still there.

Your wallet.

Your phone charger.

Even the cash in your drawer.

Nothing had been stolen.

Until you looked at your table.

Your notebook...

was gone.

Not your money.

Not your electronics.

Only your notebook.

Someone didn't want your belongings.

Someone wanted to know...

how much you had discovered.

You stood frozen in the middle of the room.

For the first time since Edward's murder...

you realized something terrifying.

You weren't just investigating a murder anymore.

Whoever killed Edward...

knew you were looking for them.

And they had just sent you a message.

**You're getting too close.**

---

# Chapter 25

### Starting Again

You couldn't sleep.

Someone had entered your apartment.

They had ignored your laptop.

Ignored your wallet.

Ignored the cash in your drawer.

They had taken only one thing.

Your notebook.

That meant two things.

First, someone knew you were investigating Edward's murder.

Second, they were worried enough to destroy your notes.

You sat at your kitchen table with a blank notebook.

Everything you had learned was gone.

You sighed.

Then you smiled.

"They took the notebook," you whispered.

"They didn't take my memory."

You opened the first page.

**Edward Langston Murder - Notebook 2**

You began writing everything you could remember.

---

# Chapter 26

### The Coffee Shop Owner

You returned to the café opposite the mansion.

The owner smiled when he saw you.

"Back again?"

"I need your help."

He poured you a cup of coffee.

"What do you remember about Friday night?"

He thought for a moment.

"I already told you about the black car."

"What else?"

"There was something strange."

"What?"

"A woman crossed the street around nine."

"A woman?"

"I couldn't see her face."

"Why not?"

"She carried a large black umbrella."

"Did she go into the mansion?"

"I don't know."

"I had customers."

You thanked him.

Another clue.

Another mystery.

---

# Chapter 27

### The Clock

You visited the mansion again.

Emma was organizing some of her father's papers.

"I found something odd," she said.

She pointed to an old grandfather clock in the hallway.

"It stopped the night Dad died."

You looked closer.

The hands pointed to exactly **9:18 p.m.**

"Did the police notice?"

"I don't think so."

The clock had stopped because its glass door had cracked.

The crack was tiny.

Almost invisible.

You wrote down the time.

9:18.

If the clock stopped during the attack...

Edward may have died earlier than the police believed.

---

# Chapter 28

### A Visit to the Museum

Ashbrook Museum had changed over the years.

New paintings.

New security systems.

New employees.

Only one thing remained the same.

The old records room.

The museum manager welcomed you politely.

"I'm looking for information about the robbery twenty-two years ago."

"The police files?"

"If possible."

The manager hesitated.

"They're public now."

He unlocked a dusty cabinet.

Inside were old reports.

Witness statements.

Photographs.

One report caught your attention.

**Employees on Duty**

Edward Langston.

Evelyn Moore.

Michael Sanders.

Richard Cole.

Grace Turner.

You copied every name.

Then another report made you stop.

A witness claimed to have seen **a woman carrying a black umbrella** leaving the museum shortly after the robbery.

Your heart skipped.

A black umbrella.

Just like the café owner described.

Coincidence?

Maybe.

---

# Chapter 29

### The Doctor

Dr. Harris had treated the Langston family for years.

You visited his clinic.

"I can't discuss private medical information," he said kindly.

"I understand."

"I only have one question."

"Go ahead."

"Did Edward seem worried before he died?"

The doctor looked away.

"I shouldn't answer."

"I'm not asking about his health."

After a long silence he spoke.

"He asked me something unusual."

"What?"

"He wanted to know if severe guilt could make someone physically sick."

You blinked.

"Guilt?"

The doctor nodded.

"He said some people carry guilt for their entire lives."

"Did he say why?"

"No."

---

# Chapter 30

### A Name Returns

That evening you searched every old newspaper you had copied.

One name appeared again.

**Michael Sanders.**

Former museum accountant.

Questioned during the robbery.

Later moved away.

No one had mentioned him.

You searched online.

Nothing.

It was as if he had disappeared.

---

# Chapter 31

### The Stranger

As you walked home, an old man sitting on a park bench called out.

"You're asking dangerous questions."

You stopped.

"Do I know you?"

"No."

"Then how do you know about me?"

He smiled.

"A small town hears everything."

He stood up slowly.

"If you're looking for the truth..."

"Stop looking at the people who hated Edward."

You frowned.

"Then who should I look at?"

He pointed toward the cemetery.

"Look at the people who loved him."

Before you could ask another question, he walked away.

You never saw him again.

---

# Chapter 32

### Olivia's Secret

You met Olivia at the mansion.

She looked exhausted.

"I found this in Edward's desk."

She handed you a small key.

"I've never seen it before."

The key had no label.

No number.

Nothing.

Together you searched the house.

Every drawer.

Every cabinet.

Nothing.

Finally Emma suggested trying the basement.

Near the back wall stood an old wooden cupboard.

The key fit perfectly.

Inside was a small metal box.

It contained several letters tied together with blue ribbon.

Every letter was addressed to Edward.

Every letter ended with the same sentence.

**"Please forgive me."**

None were signed.

---

# Chapter 33

### The Fingerprints

The police station wasn't exactly welcoming.

Detective Mason looked annoyed.

"You again?"

"I found something."

You showed him copies of the letters.

He read them quietly.

"We already have these."

"You do?"

"Yes."

"Did you find fingerprints?"

"Only Edward's."

"So the writer wore gloves?"

"Probably."

He folded the papers.

"We appreciate your enthusiasm."

"But please leave the investigation to us."

You left disappointed.

The police knew about the letters.

Yet they hadn't solved anything.

---

# Chapter 34

### James Speaks

You found James pruning roses in the garden.

"You remembered something?" you asked.

He nodded slowly.

"I wasn't sure before."

"What is it?"

"The person who visited every Friday..."

"Yes?"

"They always smelled like lavender."

"Lavender?"

"I'm certain."

You thought carefully.

Who in town regularly wore lavender perfume?

You couldn't remember.

But someone would.

---

# Chapter 35

### The Perfume Shop

Ashbrook had only one perfume shop.

The owner was cheerful and talkative.

"Lavender?"

"Oh, lots of people buy lavender."

"Can you remember any regular customers?"

She smiled.

"Mrs. Evelyn buys lavender oil every month."

"Anyone else?"

"Mrs. Wilson."

"The school principal."

"A few others."

Not enough.

Still...

You wrote Evelyn's name beside the clue.

Not because you suspected her.

Because she fit the description.

---

# Chapter 36

### The Phone Call

Late that night your phone rang.

Unknown number.

You answered.

Silence.

"Hello?"

Nothing.

Then a quiet voice.

"You're asking the wrong questions."

Before you could respond...

the call ended.

Seconds later your phone buzzed again.

A text message.

**"The necklace was never stolen."**

You stared at the screen.

The necklace...

was never stolen?

Then what happened at the museum twenty-two years ago?

Had the robbery itself been a lie?

Everything you believed suddenly fell apart.

---

# Chapter 37

### A New Theory

You spread every clue across your table.

The anonymous letters.

The museum robbery.

The hidden room.

The photograph.

The stopped clock.

The lavender perfume.

The missing notebook.

The mysterious phone call.

One thought refused to leave your mind.

What if Edward wasn't keeping newspaper clippings because he was guilty...

What if he was trying to prove something?

You wrote one sentence in large letters.

**WHAT IF THE ROBBERY NEVER HAPPENED?**

If that was true...

Edward hadn't been hiding a theft.

He had been hiding a secret.

And secrets were often more dangerous than stolen jewels.

---

## Part 4 – The Truth Begins to Surface

---

# Chapter 38

### The Necklace That Never Existed

You couldn't stop thinking about the message.

**"The necklace was never stolen."**

It sounded impossible.

For twenty-two years, everyone in Ashbrook believed the museum robbery had happened.

Newspapers reported it.

Police investigated it.

People talked about it.

How could all of them be wrong?

The next morning, you returned to the museum.

The manager looked surprised to see you again.

"Back already?"

"I need one favor."

"If I can help."

"I want to see the museum inventory from twenty-two years ago."

He frowned.

"That's an odd request."

"I know."

After several minutes, he unlocked another storage room.

Dust covered almost everything inside.

He searched through old folders before placing one on the table.

"This is everything."

You thanked him and began reading.

Every valuable item in the museum had a serial number.

Every painting.

Every statue.

Every piece of jewelry.

You searched for the famous diamond necklace.

Nothing.

You checked again.

Still nothing.

Your heartbeat became faster.

The necklace wasn't listed anywhere.

Not before the robbery.

Not after.

It had never officially belonged to the museum.

You looked at the manager.

"Are you sure these records are complete?"

"As far as I know."

You closed the file.

Someone had created a robbery around an object that officially didn't exist.

---

# Chapter 39

### Detective Mason Changes His Mind

You went straight to the police station.

Detective Mason looked tired.

"I know you're going to tell me to stop."

"I was."

You placed copies of the inventory records on his desk.

He read them once.

Then again.

Finally, he looked up.

"Where did you get these?"

"The museum."

He leaned back in his chair.

"This changes things."

"So you believe me?"

"I believe you found something we missed."

It wasn't much.

But it was the first time Detective Mason treated you like more than a curious bystander.

"If you find anything else," he said, "call me first."

You nodded.

For the first time, you weren't completely alone.

---

# Chapter 40

### The Fire

That evening you received another call from Emma.

"Please come."

Her voice was shaking.

You rushed to the mansion.

Smoke filled the backyard.

The small wooden shed behind the garden was burning.
